## Design Context

### Users
Primary users are individual short-drama creators, adaptation writers, and AI video makers turning source novels into production-ready scripts and storyboards. They work in fast iteration loops, need to move from raw text to deliverables quickly, and care more about clarity, throughput, and reusability than about playful consumer-product patterns.

### Brand Personality
Focused, cinematic, editorial.
The interface should feel dependable, sharp, and production-oriented. It should signal craft and structure rather than novelty for novelty's sake.

### Aesthetic Direction
Use an editorial studio direction: warm paper surfaces, deep ink neutrals, restrained metallic accents, and layouts that feel like a production board instead of a generic SaaS dashboard. Avoid neon gradients, default startup palettes, and anything that reads like a template-first AI tool.

### Design Principles
1. Lead with workflow clarity: every screen should make the novel-to-script-to-storyboard pipeline legible at a glance.
2. Prefer editorial hierarchy over dashboard clutter: strong typography, deliberate spacing, and a few high-signal panels beat dense card walls.
3. Keep the multi-tenant model invisible: users only manage projects, credits, and outputs; org/workspace concepts stay in the backend.
4. Optimize for decisive action: primary CTAs should move users toward projects, pricing, or sign-in without distraction.
5. Ship polished performance: responsive layouts, low visual noise, and fast-loading typography are part of product quality.
